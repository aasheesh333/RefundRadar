1. Product Definition

  • Name: Refund Radar · Tagline: "Bank owes you. We track it." / "पैसा अटका है? हम वसूली का रास्ता दिखाएँगे।"
  • What it does: Helps Indian users recover money stuck in (a) failed UPI transactions, (b) wrong/double FASTag toll deductions, (c) wrong bank charges — by calculating the compensation legally owed under RBI rules, generating ready-made complaints, tracking deadlines, and guiding escalation up to the RBI Ombudsman.
  • What it does NOT do: Handle payments, connect to bank accounts, read OTPs, file complaints automatically, give legal advice.
  • Platforms: Android + iOS from one Flutter codebase. Android first.
  • Monetization: Freemium via RevenueCat — Free: 1 active dispute + 5 starter templates only. Premium ₹99/month or ₹499/year: unlimited disputes + full 50+ complaint template library + Ombudsman letter generator + family disputes.
  • Success criteria: App builds with zero analyzer errors, all flows work offline-first, passes the QA checklist (Section 9), and is submitted to both stores (Section 10).

2.1 UPI failed transaction rules (RBI TAT Harmonisation Circular, 20 Sep 2019)

ScenarioBank's deadline (auto-reversal)Compensation if delayed
UPI P2P: account debited, beneficiary not creditedT+1 day (T = transaction date)₹100/day beyond T+1
UPI P2M (merchant): debited, confirmation not receivedT+5 days₹100/day beyond T+5
ATM: cash not dispensed but debitedT+5 days₹100/day beyond T+5
IMPS: debited, beneficiary not creditedT+1 day₹100/day beyond T+1
  • Compensation is automatic in principle — customer entitled without asking, but in practice must demand it. The counter in the app = days elapsed beyond deadline × ₹100.
  • Important honest-UX rule: money sent to a WRONG UPI ID by the user's own mistake is NOT covered — recovery depends on beneficiary consent via the bank/NPCI process. The app must set this expectation clearly and route these to a separate "wrong transfer" guidance flow.
  • NPCI chargeback window: disputes must be raised within 45 days of transaction (uniform across UPI categories since Jul 2024).
  • Update in force since 15 Jul 2025: NPCI simplified UPI chargeback processing — remitting banks can re-raise good-faith chargebacks that were earlier declined, without NPCI whitelisting. This is a bank-side change (faster resolution); the user-facing 45-day window and RBI TAT compensation rules above are UNCHANGED.

2.2 UPI escalation ladder (exact order — app's Deadline Tracker follows this)

  1. Level 1 (mandatory first): Complaint in the UPI app (GPay/PhonePe/Paytm = TPAP) → it auto-routes to the PSP bank via NPCI's UDIR system. Also/or complaint to user's own bank. Note complaint/ticket number. Wait: bank has up to 30 days.
  1. Level 2: NPCI Dispute Redressal portal: https://upihelp.npci.org.in — needs UTR/Transaction ID, amount, date, VPA, bank statement screenshot.
  1. Level 3 (after 30 days no resolution / unsatisfactory reply, within 90 days of that): RBI Ombudsman — portal https://cms.rbi.org.in (24×7, free), helpline 14448, email cpc@rbi.org.in, physical: Centralised Receipt and Processing Centre, 4th Floor, Sector 17, Chandigarh - 160017. File under "Deficiency in Service". Governed by RB-IOS 2026 (in force since 1 July 2026, supersedes RB-IOS 2021): Ombudsman can award up to ₹30 lakh for consequential loss PLUS up to ₹3 lakh additionally for loss of time, expenses, harassment/mental anguish. There is NO cap on the disputed amount itself.
  1. Level 4 (major loss): Consumer court online: https://e-jagriti.gov.in.

2.3 FASTag rules + escalation

  • Dispute window: 30 days from transaction date (NPCI NETC rules) — app must show urgency countdown.
  • Typical provider resolution timeline: 7–10 working days.
  • If false deduction is established: chargeback issued to customer + ₹1 lakh penalty on the toll operator (IHMCL policy, confirmed by IHMCL officials).
  • Blacklist rule: FASTag recharged within 10 minutes of an attempted transaction at plaza → user may be eligible for penalty refund.
  • FASTag Annual Pass (live since 15 Aug 2025): private cars/jeeps/vans only — ₹3,075 for FY 2026–27 (revised from ₹3,000 w.e.f. 1 Apr 2026), valid 200 toll crossings or 1 year (whichever first), activation ONLY via Rajmargyatra app / NHAI website. Blacklisted and Paytm FASTags are not eligible. Annual-pass wrong deductions follow the same escalation ladder below — the app should detect "annual pass active but toll still deducted" as a dispute type.
  • NPCI NETC rule effective 17 Feb 2026: FASTag transactions must be settled within a reasonable time of the vehicle crossing the plaza — late/delayed deductions long after crossing are disputable on this specific ground.
  • Escalation ladder:
    1. Issuer bank/app dispute section (bank-wise details in 2.4) against the exact transaction ID.
    1. NHAI Helpline 1033 (24×7, multilingual, covers FASTag complaints, toll charge issues) — give plaza name, lane, timestamp, vehicle no., FASTag ID.
    1. IHMCL email: falsededuction@ihmcl.com (false deductions — official chargeback channel) and etc.nodal@ihmcl.com (FASTag complaints nodal). Template in 2.6.
    1. NPCI NETC escalation: netc.org.in / NPCI NETC helpline page.
    1. RBI Ombudsman cms.rbi.org.in (FASTag issuers are RBI-regulated payment participants).

2.5 Bank charges / general banking complaints

  1. Written complaint to the bank (app/email/branch), keep ticket number. Bank window: 30 days.
  1. No reply in 30 days / rejected / unsatisfactory → RBI Ombudsman via CMS portal within 90 days: cms.rbi.org.in, helpline 14448. Free. Covers banks, NBFCs, payment participants. Compensation possible up to ₹30 lakh for consequential loss (+ up to ₹3 lakh for time/harassment) per RB-IOS 2026, in force since 1 July 2026.
  1. RBI CMS also accepts complaints for wrong/excess charges, delayed refunds, delayed fund transfers.
